5 Reasons for Choosing WordPress Over Magento for E-Commerce

When starting your e-commerce business, one of the key decisions you will make in the beginning is which platform you will be using to sell online. And while there are many possible solutions you can consider, chances are, you will be focusing on the biggest and most widely used ones. 

One of these is definitely Magento, the open source e-commerce platform that took the internet retailing world by storm in 2008. What makes Magento so widely loved among shopping cart software users is its high customizability, SEO friendliness, ability to change its themes and a very active online support community. 

All these traits actually make it very similar to another much used open source platform, WordPress, which is typically used as a content management system for blogging. But, WordPress can also be used as an e-commerce solution itself, the fact that is not as widely as known. 

eshop-Magento-vs-Wordpress

The reason why I want to talk about this is that WordPress actually blows Magento out of water in that respect in many ways and I find it baffling sometimes that so few people are aware of it. Not only it has all the above-mentioned advantages that Magento has but it also offers plenty beyond that. 

This is especially true if your main activity is content creation and let’s face it if you’re reading it, you probably are. So bear with me and take a look at what I find are the main 5 reasons WordPress rules over Magento. 

1. WORDPRESS IS MORE SEO FRIENDLY

It’s been several years since the WordCamp 2009 address of Matt Cutts, who pointed out how good of a tool WordPress is for getting better search engine rankings. But the conclusion remains true: WordPress is simply a very well built platform for publishing content that gets crawled easily by Google. 

How does this translate to e-commerce websites built in WordPress? It’s also simple. The fact that WordPress makes it easier to rank higher, your e-commerce will get a much better chance of getting picked up by the search engine spiders and in turn, you will gain much more brand impressions, clicks, and purchases. 

What is important not to overlook is to equip yourself with good SEO plugins for WordPress. The usual starting point is the WordPress SEO Plugin by Yoast – the all in one solution that handles SEO titles, meta descriptions, XML, etc. Chances are you are already using it for your WordPress blog anyway. 

2. WORDPRESS IS MORE AFFORDABLE 

Why spend more? WordPress offers much if not all of the functionality of Magento for no cost, all you need is a set of free third-party plug-ins that when added together make WordPress into a 100% viable shopping cart solution. Admittedly, Magento Community Edition is free and you can always dish out for some paid plugins on WordPress as well, but seriously, if you’re selling less than 20 items in your e-store, the free plugins are all you really need.

Additionally, you will on average end up paying more for hosting your e-commerce website built on Magento than you would for WordPress. Unless you are using Magento GO that allows you to host your store by Magento itself, but you will be forgoing much of the ability to customize, the platform won’t be open source, and you get limited storage and bandwidth. If you’re already hosting your WordPress website somewhere, you won’t have to make any additional expenses in most cases. 

3. WORDPRESS E-COMMERCE ADAPTS SEAMLESSLY TO YOUR WORDPRESS WEBSITE 

And if you are already among the thousands who use WordPress as their main publishing tool and you want to expand to selling online as well, that’s another reason to go with the plugins. The whole process of adding the e-commerce functionality to your WordPress website couldn’t be easier. 

Whether you’re selling merchandise, produce like coffee or e-books, adding the third party e-commerce plugins to your WordPress is easy as pie. There is no need to start from scratch, you are already familiar with the layout and workflow and it does not demand a degree in programming. 

4. WORDPRESS IS MUCH EASIER TO LEARN 

Both WordPress and Magento, as CMS, use a combination of Posts and Pages to form the backbone of any website. But, creating a Page in WordPress is much easier than in Magento. In the former, it takes only a few clicks to do so, while Magento requires some programming knowledge to achieve this. 

The web is crawling with good tutorials on how to start using e-commerce on WordPress so it’s easy to get the info on how to get started. Good places to start are the WordPress section of Tutsplus.com or one of the videos on lynda.com. If you are pressed with time, you can always allow a consultant to handle the setting up, and for any tips, you can find the answer in many online communities.  

5. BUYERS ARE MORE FAMILIAR WITH WORDPRESS E-COMMERCE SOLUTION

 The last but absolutely not the least is the fact that tens of thousands upon tens of thousands of buyers are very used to buying on WordPress e-commerce equipped websites. A year ago, in early 2013, there was already more than 300,000 downloads of the WordPress E-Commerce System. 

This says a lot about how widely it’s used to sell online. And buyers’ reluctance is your the enemy number one of your e-commerce. If a potential customer is met with an unknown platform which seems untrustable, he will bolt elsewhere where his doubts will be assuaged. 

 There are certain situations when Magento will truly be the winner and be better suited for your e-commerce needs, such as when you have a vast number of products on offer, when you have time and skills (or can pay someone who does) to tackle the more advanced-level setting up of the store, when you are starting completely from scratch and when your content publishing takes a much less important role in your business model. 

 But in most other cases, I would recommend you choose WP e-commerce as your platform for choice as it will save you effort, money and potentially, from losing your customers.
