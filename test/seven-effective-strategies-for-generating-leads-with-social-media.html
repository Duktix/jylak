7 Effective Strategies for Generating Leads with Social Media

Whether your business is primarily B2C or B2B oriented, social media can and should play an important role in your lead generation strategy. Just the fact that you’re present on social media is not enough. The choice of tools is pretty comprehensive but your own mix will depend on which social networks you are focusing on. Whichever you choose the basic idea is to give off something of value in exchange for people’s contact info or endorsement. Let’s take a look at some of the most widely used and most effective strategies. 


1. CONTESTS:

The bread and butter of any lead generation, contests are typically used by B2C businesses on similarly oriented social networks such as Facebook, Pinterest or Instagram. Vast majority of them are photo contests as these are especially visual social media. The idea is to create a concept for the contest that brings together your brand and engage users to contribute with their real life stories and creative takes on what role your brand plays in their life. Or, you can focus on the events that are currently taking place such as holidays and seasons. Another important element is choosing the appropriate prizes in order to incentive the users and stand out from other contests. Make sure to research the best app to used to launch the contest as well — meaning you will take into account pricing, features, stability and most of all: the ability to display your campaign on mobile as well. 

 2. WEBINARS:

Webinars are typically half-hour to hour long online sessions where one or a few experts give an insightful, in-depth look on a topic. Typically, used by B2B businesses, they can attract some very valuable leads because they are non-intrusive and the users are already probably very interested in your area of expertise or niche. Normally they are given through specialized webinar tools but as of late, more and more brands organize them directly through their social media. Tools such as 22social.com or Google Hangouts allow you to host webinars right from your social media profile, gaining from the synergy of the two. 

3. INFLUENCER:

OUTREACH Just like blogger outreach, this tactic focuses on identifying the key influencers in your industry and creating a relationship with them through interacting regularly and with good will through your social media. This is normally best done through Twitter, LinkedIn and other niche social media such as Quora, for instance. You can use a tool such as Topsy.com or Inkybee.com to narrow down your focus to industry influencers with the best and biggest reach and then start interacting with them. On Twitter, this means replying often to their tweets, favoring them and retweeting them. On LinkedIn, this means joining the same groups as them and participating in same discussions as them. Be patient, and not to pushy and gauge the right moment. Only then can you contact them directly through a message or an email and ask for a possibility of partnership or present them with your product/service. 

4. CONTENT CREATION:

Some businesses can’t afford to create a regular flow of original content, whether these are funny images for Facebook or white papers or info graphics. Instead of using resources to do that, they focus on finding the best content already out there. This is called content creation and has been the weapon of choice for some of the most popular individuals and businesses on social media such as Guy Kawasaki or Mashable. How do you find content to curate? Make yourself at home at Reddit or Digg, or use some of the more advanced platforms such as Curata. Other than that, follow the golden rules of social media publishing: adapt your message and frequency of posting to each social network and be consistent, but don’t be afraid to try out new things. 

5. FACEBOOK GROUPS AND GOOGLE PLUS COMMUNITIES:

It’s true Facebook is best for B2C and post-sales but it can also be used as a decent B2B weapon. I’m talking about Facebook groups, the underestimated little feature overshadowed by Facebook pages. Although you can join them only with your personal profile, they can bring you closer to a treasure trove of potentials leads. Coupled with Facebook’s new Graph Search, you can really narrow down on specific groups effectively. Groups are especially good for reaching leads who combine B2C and B2B, such as consultants, public figures and entrepreneur. A hidden feature of groups is the ability to invite fellow members to your Facebook event promoting a certain campaign that ultimately leads to a landing page. Powerful stuff. Similarly, Google Plus communities allow you to interact and engage directly the large number of Google Plus users, that is its predominantly male, tech-minded audience. 

6. INFOGRAPHICS:

Infographics make for one of the most widely used lead generation method and at the same time, one of the most challenging pulling off well. In their essence, infographics are a visual representation of interesting data about a certain topic. They combine charts, illustrations and text with graphic design to tell a story in a clear, easily understood and striking way. You could say that they reached their peak in popularity around 2012 but they are still a very powerful instrument for internet marketers. There’s luckily a number of tools out there that make the whole process of creating infographics easier. You can find a good list of these tools in this article. 

7. VIDEO:

Making a good video is still an art in itself, even though the ability to shoot one is available today to anyone with a smartphone. Haphazardly created videos are unlikely to serve your goals, while cheap production values can also alienate people from you or your brand. Still, it’s not necessary to spend thousands of dollars on professional videos. If you are choosing a video with a talking head, make sure you have plenty of good lighting and use a standalone mic to record your voice. Nothing’s more grating that barely audible voice in a video trying to sell something. You can easily edit videos even with your phone or tablet into a coherent whole with the recently updated version of YouTube Capture app. If you are looking for a simple animation with text, you can create a presentation on SlideShare and then make into a video that you can upload to YouTube or another video sharing platform. Last, but not the least, make sure to create a script for your video and turn it into a storyboard before you even start creating it!
